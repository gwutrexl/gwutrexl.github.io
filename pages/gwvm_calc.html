To help you with your disk sizing:

The DCO Windows group has a standard 50G OS disk and a 2-10G pagefile disk (depending on the memory size). They recommend production servers have these OS disks on the high-performance tier.

The DCO Unix team generally starts with a 40G OS and swap space, depending on the operating system in use. All disks are generally placed on the standard-performance tier unless high-perf is requested.

For any OS, application data is not housed on the OS disk and should be on separate virtual disk(s). These disks can be on either the standard or high performance tiers.

Workloads with more than 4 vCPU configured are available based on demonstrated resource need. Your workload will perform better with higher utilization of less vCPU than idle of many vCPU. Please feel free to reach out to the virtual hosting team to "right size" your servers.
